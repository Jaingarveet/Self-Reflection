Advice:
 - Always read the passage carefully and identify the main topic.
 - Always identify the key points that support the main topic.
 - Always identify the specific examples that are used to illustrate the key points.
 - Always identify the author's purpose in writing the passage.
 - Always identify the author's assumptions about the reader.
 - Always identify the author's tone and style.
 - Always identify the author's use of evidence and reasoning.
 - Always identify the author's conclusions and implications.
 - Always be aware of your own biases and assumptions.
 - Always be willing to revise your understanding of the passage as you read it.
 - Always be willing to ask questions about the passage.
