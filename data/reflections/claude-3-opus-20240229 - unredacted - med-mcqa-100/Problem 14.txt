I incorrectly identified the end products of anaerobic glycolysis. While I correctly stated that glycolysis produces 2 ATP and 2 NADH, I failed to account for the fact that in anaerobic conditions, the NADH is oxidized back to NAD+ to allow glycolysis to continue. My knowledge gap was in understanding the regeneration of NAD+ in anaerobic conditions.
Error Keywords:
 - Incomplete understanding of anaerobic glycolysis
 - Failure to account for NAD+ regeneration
 - Confusion about end products in anaerobic conditions
Solution:
1. Glycolysis is the metabolic pathway that converts glucose into pyruvate.
2. Glycolysis occurs in both aerobic and anaerobic conditions.
3. In aerobic conditions, pyruvate enters the citric acid cycle and electron transport chain.
4. In anaerobic conditions, pyruvate is converted to lactate to regenerate NAD+.
5. During glycolysis, there is a net production of 2 ATP molecules.
6. In the glycolysis pathway, there are two steps where NAD+ is reduced to NADH.
7. In anaerobic conditions, the NADH must be oxidized back to NAD+ to allow glycolysis to continue.
8. The oxidation of NADH to NAD+ is coupled with the reduction of pyruvate to lactate.
9. Therefore, in anaerobic glycolysis, the end products are 2 ATP molecules and 2 NAD+ molecules.
