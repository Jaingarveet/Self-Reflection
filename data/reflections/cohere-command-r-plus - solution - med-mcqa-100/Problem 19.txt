Red infarcts, also known as hemorrhagic infarcts, refer to areas of bleeding within an organ due to a blockage in the blood supply. While red infarcts can occur in various organs, the correct answer specifically refers to the lung.

The presence of red infarcts in the lung is indicative of a unique pathologic process called pulmonary infarction. It occurs when a pulmonary embolism, which is a blockage in the pulmonary artery or one of its branches, interrupts the blood supply to a portion of the lung tissue. As a result, the affected area of the lung becomes infarcted, and the characteristic red infarct can be observed.
