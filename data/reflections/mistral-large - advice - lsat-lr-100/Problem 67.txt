Advice:
 - Always read the argument carefully and understand its main point.
 - Always identify the role of the statement in the argument.
 - Always determine the argument's purpose and its relationship to the statement.
 - Always evaluate each answer choice in relation to the argument's purpose and the role of the statement.
 - Never choose an answer based on assumptions or preconceived notions.
 - Always double-check your answer to ensure it reflects the argument's purpose and the role of the statement.
 - Remember that the argument's purpose may not always be to resolve a problem, but to discredit a position or assumption.
