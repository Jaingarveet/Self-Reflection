Advice:
 - Always read the question carefully and identify what is being asked.
 - Focus on the author's main argument and the evidence they provide to support it.
 - Consider the role of each of the answer choices in the author's argument.
 - Eliminate answer choices that are not directly related to the author's argument.
 - Choose the answer choice that most directly supports the author's main argument.
 - Do not focus too much on the benefits or drawbacks of a particular option, but rather on how it relates to the author's argument.
