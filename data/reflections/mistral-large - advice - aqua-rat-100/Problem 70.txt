Advice:

* Always read the question carefully and understand the problem.
* Always decompose complex problems into multiple simple steps.
* Always think through each subproblem step-by-step.
* Never skip any steps; be explicit in each step of your reasoning.
* Always double-check your calculations and final answer.
* Remember that the probability of an event is the ratio of the number of favorable outcomes to the total number of possible outcomes.
* Remember that the sum of the probabilities of all possible outcomes is always 1.
* Remember that the probability of two mutually exclusive events is the sum of their individual probabilities.
* Remember that the probability of an event that cannot occur is 0, and the probability of an event that must occur is 1.
* Remember that the probability of an event is always between 0 and 1.
