Advice:
 - Always read the question carefully and understand the problem.
 - Always consider the specific symptoms presented by the patient.
 - Always decompose complex problems into multiple simple steps.
 - Always think through each subproblem step-by-step.
 - Never skip any steps; be explicit in each step of your reasoning.
 - Always double-check your analysis and final answer.
 - Remember that the functions of nerves can overlap, and injury to one nerve can cause symptoms similar to injury to another nerve.
 - Consider the clinical presentation of nerve injuries, not just the functions of the nerves.
