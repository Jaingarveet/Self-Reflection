Solution:
The cavernous part of the internal carotid artery is located in the middle cranial fossa and has several branches.
The inferior hypophyseal artery is a branch of the cavernous part of the internal carotid artery.
The ophthalmic artery is also a branch of the cavernous part of the internal carotid artery.
The meningeal artery is not a branch of the cavernous part of the internal carotid artery.
