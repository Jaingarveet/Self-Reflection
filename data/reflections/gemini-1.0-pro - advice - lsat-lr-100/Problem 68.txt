Advice:
- Always read the question carefully and understand the argument.
- Identify the key issue in the argument.
- Determine what information would be most useful in evaluating the argument.
- Consider all of the answer choices and choose the one that provides the most relevant information.
- Be sure to consider all of the factors that could affect the argument, both positive and negative.
- Do not focus on irrelevant information or make assumptions that are not supported by the evidence.
