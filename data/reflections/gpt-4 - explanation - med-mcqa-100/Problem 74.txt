I answered the question incorrectly because I misunderstood the role of the Superior Laryngeal Nerve (SLN) in swallowing. While it's true that the SLN is involved in voice production, it also plays a significant role in the swallowing mechanism. The SLN innervates the cricopharyngeus muscle, which is a part of the upper esophageal sphincter. Dysfunction of this muscle due to SLN injury can lead to aspiration.
