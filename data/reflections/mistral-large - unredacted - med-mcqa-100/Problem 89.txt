Solution:
Spondylolisthesis is a condition in which one vertebra slips forward over the one below it.
Anteroposterior X-ray is not as useful as lateral view X-ray in diagnosing spondylolisthesis.
Lateral view X-ray is useful for diagnosing spondylolisthesis and assessing the degree of slippage.
CT scans can provide more detailed images of the vertebrae and can help identify any bony abnormalities.
MRI scans can provide detailed images of the soft tissues, including the spinal cord and nerves, and can help identify any compression or damage to these structures.
