**Explanation:** I focused on the assumption that the brothers' presence was necessary for giving the gift, neglecting the broader logical flaw. The argument concludes that *some* brothers must have been present based on evidence that *at least one* was involved in the gift.
**Error Keywords:**
*   Misidentification of logical fallacy
*   Focusing on a specific detail rather than the overall argument structure
*   Overlooking the scope of the conclusion
**Step-by-Step Solution:**
1. **Identify the conclusion:** At least some of Hana's brothers were at the party.
2. **Identify the evidence:** The brothers planned to give a specific gift that Hana received.
3. **Analyze the gap:** The evidence only shows one or more brothers were involved with the gift, not necessarily present. 
4. **Recognize the fallacy:** The argument assumes what is true for *some* (involved in the gift) is true for *only those* (present at the party). This is a fallacy of **undistributed middle**.
**Instructions for Future:**
1. **Identify the conclusion and evidence clearly.**
2. **Focus on the link between evidence and conclusion, not just specific details.**
3. **Consider alternative explanations for the evidence that don't support the conclusion.**
4. **Be familiar with common logical fallacies, including undistributed middle.**
**General Advice:**
*   **Practice identifying logical fallacies in various arguments.**
*   **Pay attention to the scope of claims and evidence (all, some, none).**
*   **Don't get distracted by irrelevant details; focus on the core logic.**
*   **Consider multiple perspectives and possibilities before drawing conclusions.**
