## Step-by-Step Solution
1.  **Iron Deficiency Anemia:** This condition is characterized by low iron levels.
2.  **TIBC (Total Iron Binding Capacity):** In response to low iron, the body increases its capacity to bind iron, leading to **increased TIBC**.
3.  **Serum Ferritin:** Ferritin stores iron, so low iron levels result in **decreased serum ferritin**.
4.  **Transferrin Saturation:** This is the percentage of iron-binding sites on transferrin that are occupied by iron. It is calculated as (Serum Iron / TIBC) x 100. In iron deficiency anemia, transferrin saturation is **less than 15%**.
5.  **Transferrin Ferritin Index:** This is calculated as TIBC / Serum Ferritin. Since TIBC is high and serum ferritin is low in iron deficiency anemia, the index will be **greater than 2**.
Therefore, the diagnostic findings for iron deficiency anemia are: **Increased TIBC, decreased serum ferritin, and Transferrin ferritin index > 2 (Choice A)**.
