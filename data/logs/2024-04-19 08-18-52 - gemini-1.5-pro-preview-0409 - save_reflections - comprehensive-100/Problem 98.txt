**Explanation:**
My knowledge base incorrectly assumed that all amino acids require aminoacyl-tRNA for incorporation into proteins.  This is not true for post-translational modifications like hydroxylysine.
**Error Keywords:**
*   Factual error

## Step-by-Step Solution
1.  **Recall the role of aminoacyl-tRNA:** It carries amino acids to the ribosome for protein synthesis during translation.
2.  **Consider post-translational modifications:** These occur after protein synthesis and may not require aminoacyl-tRNA.
3.  **Analyze hydroxylysine:** It is formed by the hydroxylation of lysine residues in collagen after translation.
4.  **Conclusion:** Hydroxylysine does not require aminoacyl-tRNA for its incorporation as it is a post-translational modification. 
## Instructions for Future Problem Solving
